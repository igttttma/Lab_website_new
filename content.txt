Optoelectronic HCI Lab
PHOENIX Lab 
Light-Driven Matter, Interfaces, and Future Technologies
Phoenix Lab takes an interdisciplinary approach to exploring optical technologies as a generative medium for reimagining matter, interfaces, energy, and future interactive systems. At the intersection of Optical Technologies and Human-Computer Interaction, we create programmable materials, physical interfaces, robotic and energy systems, and AI-enabled experiences that reshape how people interact with and through the physical world.
Phoenix Lab is led by Prof. Yuhua Jin at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen.

Photo-Chromeleon
Recolor physical objects like a chameleon using light.
A reprogrammable color-changing material system that allows physical objects to be digitally recolored after fabrication. By projecting light onto coated objects, users can write, erase, and update color patterns on real-world surfaces.

Join Us:

Join PHOENIX Lab
PHOENIX Lab welcomes curious, creative, and hands-on researchers who are excited about light-driven matter, interfaces, energy systems, and future interactive technologies.
We are an interdisciplinary lab at the intersection of optical technologies and human-computer interaction. We welcome people from diverse backgrounds, including HCI, computer science, optical engineering, electronic engineering, materials science, mechanical engineering, robotics, design, and related fields. If you are interested in building new physical systems, exploring unconventional ideas, and creating technologies for the yet-to-come future, we would be happy to hear from you.
Navigate: Postdocs | PhD & MPhil | Research Assistants & Interns | How to Apply

Postdoctoral Researchers
We are recruiting postdoctoral researchers interested in interdisciplinary research related to light-driven materials, programmable matter, optical systems, physical interfaces, robotic systems, energy systems, fabrication, AI-enabled interactive systems, and human-computer interaction.
Strong candidates may have expertise in one or more of the following areas:
Optical engineering, photonics, or optoelectronic systems
Human-computer interaction, interactive systems, or physical computing
Photoresponsive materials, programmable materials, or smart materials
Digital fabrication, mechanical design, or prototyping
Robotics, sensing, actuation, or energy systems
Computational design, AI-enabled systems, or creative computing
Applicants with strong independent research ability, hands-on prototyping skills, and a publication record in related areas are especially encouraged to apply. Candidates interested in applying for external fellowships or university-level postdoctoral funding are also welcome to reach out to discuss potential research directions.

PhD and MPhil Students
We welcome prospective PhD and MPhil students who are interested in creating future physical and interactive technologies at the intersection of optical technologies and human-computer interaction.
Students with backgrounds in HCI, computer science, electronic engineering, optical engineering, materials science, mechanical engineering, robotics, design, or related fields are encouraged to apply. We especially value applicants who combine technical depth with imagination, curiosity, and the willingness to work across disciplinary boundaries.
Prospective students are encouraged to contact Prof. Yuhua Jin before applying through the official university application system.

Research Assistants & Interns
We welcome research assistants, visiting students, undergraduate interns, and master’s interns who are interested in hands-on interdisciplinary research.
Depending on the project, students may contribute to optical experiments, material systems, interactive prototypes, hardware design, robotic platforms, software development, AI-enabled interaction, user studies, video production, or visual documentation.
Longer-term commitments are preferred, especially for projects that require experimental training, system building, or iterative prototyping.

Wildcard
If your background is not listed above but you see a strong and creative connection with PHOENIX Lab, please feel free to reach out. We are always open to unusual combinations of ideas, skills, and disciplines.

How to Apply
Interested applicants may email Prof. Yuhua Jin with the following materials:
A brief introduction of your background and research interests
Your CV
Representative publications, portfolios, project videos, GitHub links, or design works, if available
Transcripts, if applicable
A short explanation of why you are interested in PHOENIX Lab
